### D.2 How to Explain FREEZE vs. Roadmap

**Language to Use**:
- "v2-FROZEN" = Production-stable, interfaces locked, actively operational
- "Demo-Ready" = Full demo with storylines, backend may be simplified
- "Backend-Ready" = Full database persistence, production-grade
- "Pending" = Future roadmap, not current priority

**Never Say**:
- "Coming soon" (implies not built)
- "Beta" (implies instability)
- "MVP" (implies incomplete)

## PART E: GOVERNANCE & TRUST MESSAGING

### E.1 Governance-First Philosophy

**Core Message**:

> "At WebWaka, governance isn't a feature. It's architecture.
>
> Every platform instance, every transaction, every record is built on a foundation of audit-readiness, commerce isolation, and jurisdictional awareness. This isn't compliance as afterthought—it's infrastructure design."

### E.2 Why FREEZE Matters

**Message for Technical Audiences**:

> "A v2-FROZEN suite means the interface contract is locked. Partners can depend on stability. Clients can depend on consistency. Regulators can depend on predictability.
>
> FREEZE doesn't mean abandoned. It means mature. Changes are additive, versioned, and governed."

**Message for Business Audiences**:

> "When we say a suite is FROZEN, we mean it's production-ready and stable. Your investment in training, configuration, and client deployment is protected. We don't break what works."

### E.3 Why Commerce Boundary Matters

**Core Message**:

> "In WebWaka, money flows through one place: the Commerce Suite.
>
> Industry verticals—Education, Health, Logistics, Church—emit 'facts' about what happened: a fee was owed, a service was rendered, a tithe was given. They never touch payment processing, wallet management, or accounting.
>
> This Commerce Boundary means auditors see one clean financial layer. No shadow ledgers in Education. No rogue payment flows in Logistics. One truth."

### E.4 Why Append-Only Matters

**Core Message**:

> "Audit logs on WebWaka are append-only. Evidence bundles are cryptographically sealed. Financial facts are immutable.
>
> You cannot delete a donation record. You cannot modify an attendance log. You cannot erase a governance decision.
>
> This isn't a limitation. It's a guarantee."

### E.5 Why WebWaka is Regulator-Friendly

**Message for Regulators**:

> "WebWaka is designed with regulators in mind:
>
> - **Regulator Access Logs**: Every external inspection is logged with timestamp, IP, and purpose
> - **Transparency Reports**: Publishable compliance summaries per organization
> - **Commerce Isolation**: All financial activity in one auditable layer
> - **Jurisdictional Awareness**: Nigeria-first defaults (CAC, FIRS, sector regulations)
> - **No Hidden Data**: Append-only architecture means complete audit trails
>
> We don't make it hard to regulate. We make it easy."
